Description

In this stage, we will try to solve our classification problem using logistic regression. Then, let's add two metrics to the model: Accuracy and AUC (Area Under the Curve). Accuracy is the proportion of the correct predictions to their total number. AUC is the measure of a classifier's ability to distinguish between classes. It is used as a summary of the Receiver Operating Characteristic (ROC) curve. For more information, study the Understanding AUC - ROC Curve article by Sarang Narkhede. We recommend ready-made implementations of the model and metrics from sklearn.linear_model and sklearn.metrics, respectively.

Objectives

Welcome to one day in the life of a data scientist: building and scoring models. Ready, steady, go:

  1. Construct an object of the LogisticRegression class. Use the solver='liblinear' parameter;
  2. Fit your model on the training feature matrix you got during the previous stage;
  3. Calculate the Accuracy and AUC score of your model on the test set;
  4. As a result, output two values: Accuracy and AUC score on the test set. Print each number on a separate line.
Example

An example of the program output:
