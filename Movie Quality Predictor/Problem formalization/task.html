Description

We will work with the Large Movie Review Dataset. This dataset was collected in association with the Learning Word Vectors for Sentiment Analysis paper. It has a wide range of metadata, including cast, crew, plot keywords, budget, posters, release dates, languages, production companies, and countries in movie reviews. In our case, the reviews are not just the opinions of viewers. We also take the comments of movie directors and announcements into account. Some of these reviews are available before the movie's premiere. Also, we have ratings from 0 to 10 for all films in the training data. Our goal is to build a model for predicting which movie will have higher ratings after analyzing the reviews with metadata.

The whole point is that we can get that kind of metadata before the movie premiere! So, video hosting services can buy broadcasting rights only for the best movies, or work on their marketing campaigns if the predictions are not very promising.

By predicting the ratings from 0 to 10, we can solve the regression problem. But is there any difference between movies with ratings of 2.72 or 3.14? Both movies are bad, so they are not a good fit for our video hosting platform. Tastes differ and making one universal system for all countries, ages, and personal preferences is impossible. But both movies rated 8.91 and 9.18 are good enough for our platform. So the problem becomes a classification problem. To solve it, let's tag movies with ratings > 7 as "good" and with movie ratings < 5 as "bad." Other data isn't relevant to us.
